# Music School Institutional Adoption — Outreach Plan

Target 3-5 music schools/colleges that could use Demoify for demo submission workflows, student feedback, and collaborative projects.

## Target Schools

### 1. Berklee College of Music (Boston, MA)
- **Why:** Largest independent music college globally. Already has an internal "Berklee Online" submission system.
- **Angle:** Replace email-based demo submissions with permanent URL-based versioned sharing. Faculty can leave timestamped feedback on student compositions.
- **Contact Path:** Faculty directory → production/engineering professors → IT admin → pilot program
- **Coupon Code:** MUSICSCHOOL (1000 credits per student, 50 redemptions)
- **Pitch:**
  > "Students submit demos via email/Dropbox with no version history. Demoify gives every submission a permanent URL, preserves every version, and lets faculty leave timestamped feedback. Free for pilot this semester."

### 2. USC Thornton School of Music (Los Angeles, CA)
- **Why:** Major music industry school in LA. Strong recording/production program. Industry connections.
- **Angle:** Prepare students for real-world collaboration workflows. Version control is standard in every other creative field — music should be too.
- **Contact Path:** Music Technology dept → Dr. Richard McIlvery (Recording Arts) → pilot with one class
- **Alternate Contact:** Associate Dean of Academic Programs

### 3. NYU Steinhardt — Music Technology (New York, NY)
- **Why:** Leading music technology program. Strong emphasis on music production + tech crossover. Students are already developers-musicians.
- **Angle:** The Git mental model resonates with their tech-oriented curriculum. Use Demoify as a teaching tool for both music collaboration AND version control concepts.
- **Contact Path:** Music Technology faculty → Prof. Agnieszka Roginska (chair) → suggest as course tool
- **Pitch:**
  > "Your music tech students understand Git. Show them the same principle applied to their own craft — versioned song sharing with timestamped feedback. Open source, so they can even contribute to the platform itself."

### 4. Belmont University — Curb College (Nashville, TN)
- **Why:** Major music business school in Nashville. Strong songwriting and production programs.
- **Angle:** Songwriting collaboration is their bread and butter. Students co-write across cohorts — Demoify gives them a shared workspace with version history.
- **Contact Path:** Songwriting dept → Dr. Steven Jesse Bernstein → demo for co-writing lab

### 5. University of Miami — Frost School of Music (Miami, FL)
- **Why:** Strong music engineering and production programs. Known for innovative music technology approaches.
- **Angle:** Their "Frost Music Live" streaming program could use Demoify for collaborative production tracking.
- **Contact Path:** Music Engineering Technology → Prof. Colby Leider → proposal

### 6. Australian Institute of Music (Sydney, Australia — Backup option)
- **Why:** International reach. Strong remote collaboration needs.
- **Contact Path:** Head of Contemporary Music Performance → pilot program

## Outreach Email Template

**Subject:** Versioned demo sharing for [School Name] — free pilot

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

I'm the developer of Demoify (<https://demoify.app>), a free, open-source platform for versioned demo sharing — think GitHub for songs.

I'm reaching out because [School Name]'s [Dept Name] program could significantly benefit from replacing the email/Dropbox demo workflow with permanent, versioned sharing links.

Here's what Demoify gives your students and faculty:

1. **One link per submission, forever** — students upload a demo and get a permanent URL. Upload a new version? Same URL serves the latest track; old versions stay accessible.
2. **Timestamped feedback** — faculty and peers leave comments anchored to specific moments in the track ("at 2:30, the bass needs more presence").
3. **Private by default** — songs can be shared only with band members or made public for showcase.
4. **Free to use** — upload credits included (I'll provide each student with 1,000 credits via coupon code).
5. **Open source** — students can even contribute to the codebase as a learning exercise.

I'd love to set up a semester-long pilot program — no cost, no commitment. Happy to jump on a call to discuss.

Best,
[sender name]

## Pilot Program Details

**What we offer:**
- Custom coupon codes for students (1,000 free credits each)
- Setup guide for faculty (how to create assignments using Demoify links)
- Optional guest lecture on version control for music (remote, 20 min)
- No cost, no contract

**What we ask:**
- Faculty encourages use in one class/project
- Students provide feedback on the platform (what's broken, what's missing)
- Option to share case study for Demoify's website
